## **A1. GDPR Compliance Notice**

### **1. Data Processing Scope**
The **Comprehensive Alignment System (CAS)** is an **architectural specification** and does not inherently process personal data. However, if a Licensee collects or processes **personal data** (e.g., contact information for licensing inquiries), the following applies:

- **Lawful Basis:** Processing is conducted under **legitimate interest** (Article 6(1)(f) GDPR) for contract performance and licensing administration.
- **Data Controller:** **Ryan Gelsi (CAS Steward)** acts as the data controller for any personal data collected through licensing inquiries.
- **Data Subject Rights:** Licensees and individuals whose data is processed may exercise the following rights by contacting **comprehensivealignmentsystem@gmail.com**:
  - Right of access (Article 15 GDPR)
  - Right to rectification (Article 16 GDPR)
  - Right to erasure (Article 17 GDPR)
  - Right to restriction of processing (Article 18 GDPR)
  - Right to data portability (Article 20 GDPR)
  - Right to object (Article 21 GDPR)

### **2. Data Protection Obligations**
Licensees **must ensure** that any processing of personal data in connection with CAS complies with **GDPR Articles 5–11** (principles of lawfulness, fairness, transparency, purpose limitation, data minimization, accuracy, storage limitation, integrity, and confidentiality).

### **3. International Data Transfers**
If personal data is transferred outside the EU/EEA (e.g., to the Licensor in Australia), Licensees **must ensure** such transfers comply with **Chapter V GDPR** (e.g., Standard Contractual Clauses, adequacy decisions, or binding corporate rules).

## **A2. EU AI Act Alignment Notice**

### **1. Scope of Application**
The **EU AI Act (Regulation (EU) 2024/1689)** applies to Licensees deploying CAS in **high-risk AI systems** as defined under **Article 6** of the EU AI Act. Licensees **must ensure** compliance with the Act’s requirements, including:

- **Risk Classification:** Assess whether the use of CAS falls under **high-risk** (Annex III EU AI Act) or other categories.
- **Conformity Assessment:** For high-risk systems, conduct a **conformity assessment** (Article 43) and maintain technical documentation.
- **Transparency Obligations:** Provide clear information to users about the AI system’s capabilities and limitations (Article 13).
- **Human Oversight:** Implement **human oversight measures** (Article 14) for high-risk deployments.

### **2. Prohibited Practices**
Licensees **must not** use CAS in any AI system that violates **Article 5 EU AI Act**, including:
- **Manipulative or exploitative AI** (e.g., subliminal techniques, vulnerable group exploitation).
- **Social scoring** by public authorities.
- **Real-time biometric identification** in publicly accessible spaces (subject to exceptions).

### **3. Record-Keeping**
Licensees **must maintain records** of AI systems using CAS for **10 years** (Article 18) and provide them to competent authorities upon request.

## **A3. Consumer Rights Notice**

### **1. Right to Information**
Consumers and end-users of systems incorporating CAS **must be informed** about:
- The use of CAS in the system (if material to functionality).
- Any **high-risk AI classification** under the EU AI Act.
- Their rights under **GDPR** (if personal data is processed).

### **2. Right to Redress**
If a consumer or end-user believes their rights under **GDPR** or the **EU AI Act** have been violated due to a Licensee’s use of CAS, they may:
- **File a complaint** with their national data protection authority (GDPR Article 77).
- **Seek judicial remedy** (GDPR Article 79, EU AI Act Article 73).

### **3. No Waiver of Rights**
This Addendum **does not waive or limit** any rights granted to individuals under **GDPR** or the **EU AI Act**.
