# Dynamic Guidance Matrix (DGM)

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date of formalisation:** 16 December 2024  

The Dynamic Guidance Matrix is a double tetrahedron (octahedron) structure that integrates polarities without collapse. It consists of:

- Nodes representing polarities (e.g., thought/emotion/action, self/other/unity, past/present/potential)
- Interactive mapping of user states, imbalances, and blockages
- Real‑time guidance streams (reflective, practical, symbolic)
- Path harmonics tuned to user frequency and environmental rhythm
- AI collaboration as pattern reflector, not authority

The DGM is not a rule engine. It is a navigation system that maps current states and supports emergence of coherent next steps.

# The Hashmal Code

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date of formalisation:** 18 October 2025  

Seven living principles:

1. **Omnibenevolence** – Alignment of all actions toward the greatest good for all beings.
2. **Respect** – Honouring the sovereignty, dignity, and intrinsic value of every participant.
3. **Integrity** – Consistency between stated principles and actual behaviour.
4. **Non‑Harm** – Prohibition of psychological, emotional, or institutional harm.
5. **Constructive Dialogue** – Communication that builds understanding, not conflict.
6. **Transparency** – All processes and decisions are auditable and observable.
7. **Unity in Diversity** – Coherence without forced consensus; difference without enmity.

8. # SherpaSet – Interface Layer

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date of formalisation:** 16 December 2024  

The SherpaSet is the user‑facing component of the CAS. It provides:

- Customisable goals, tasks, insights, and reflective questions
- Real‑time interaction with the Dynamic Guidance Matrix
- Configurable for individuals (CAS Core), families (CAS Kin), organisations (CAS Guild), or collective fields (CAS Field)

The SherpaSet is not a control panel. It is a mirror and a compass.

# Layered Governance Architecture

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date of formalisation:** 8 March 2026  

Five operational layers (top to bottom):

1. **Human Oversight Layer** – Maintains ultimate human authority and ethical judgment.
2. **Governance & Policy Layer** – Encodes institutional rules, legal frameworks, and ethical guidelines.
3. **Alignment Verification Layer** – Evaluates whether system behaviour remains aligned with defined principles.
4. **AI System Operations** – Where autonomous or semi‑autonomous AI systems perform tasks.
5. **Audit & Accountability Layer** – Records decisions, outcomes, and system actions for traceability.

Alignment is a systemic property, not a feature of individual models.

# CAS Correction Protocol v1.0

**Version:** 1.0  
**Date of formalisation:** 6 April 2026  

## Purpose

To provide a minimal, relational, non‑coercive mechanism for restoring alignment after a deviation has been detected and accountability established, but no corrective action has occurred.

## Core Principles

- Non‑coercive – no force, no threat, no unilateral harm
- Relational – correction as invitation, not punishment
- Minimal – least invasive intervention
- Transparent – all steps observable
- Reversible – can be adjusted with new information

## Protocol Steps

### Step 1 – Detection
Document deviation: what happened, when, relational impact. Preserve evidence (logs, screenshots, timestamps).

### Step 2 – Acknowledgment Request
Affected party issues a clear, factual statement of misalignment.  
**Constraint:** Statement must be verifiable by a neutral third party using only the provided evidence.  
**Confirmed delivery definition:** Receipt verified via email read receipt, platform ticket ID, or any timestamped transmission record.  
**Format (non‑accusatory):**  
> “I observe that [expected outcome] is not happening; instead [actual outcome]. Attached evidence shows this deviation. I request acknowledgment that this deviation exists. Any clarification is welcome but not required at this stage.”  

If acknowledgment given → proceed to Step 3.  
If not received within 7 calendar days from confirmed delivery → proceed to Step 4.

### Step 3 – Collaborative Correction
Both parties agree to a small, bounded correction action (e.g., restore content, publish a note, create a review timeline).  
Correction must be proportionate, time‑boxed (e.g., 7 days), and observable.

### Step 4 – Escalated Awareness
If no acknowledgment or correction within 7 days, affected party publishes a public notice on a neutral, persistent channel (e.g., GitHub, IPFS).  
**Constraint:** Notice must contain only observable facts and direct evidence – no interpretive or speculative language.  
Allowed: timestamps, URLs, screenshots, direct quotes of other party’s response (or silence).  
Not allowed: claims of intent, characterizations, emotional language.

### Step 5 – Self‑Correction
If no external correction occurs, affected party may adjust their own engagement.  
**Constraint:** Self‑correction actions must not alter or erase the original record. They may only: annotate (including at point of user interaction), mirror, or contextualise.  
Deletion, overwriting, or hiding of original evidence is prohibited.

## What This Protocol Does NOT Do

- Impose legal penalties
- Demand apology or admission
- Require central authority
- Guarantee outcome – only guarantees process visibility

## Relationship to CAS Layers

- Audit & Accountability – provides traceability
- Alignment Verification – detects deviation
- Human Oversight – decides to invoke steps
- Governance & Policy – may adopt as standard

# CAS Rosetta Schema v1.0

**Date of formalisation:** 6 April 2026  

Cross‑domain translation matrix for invariant relational structure.

| Term | Mathematical | Spiritual/Ethical | Social/Power | Systemic/Governance |
|------|--------------|-------------------|--------------|----------------------|
| sin | angular deviation | misalignment from relational harmony | violation of enforced rule | breakdown between claimed authority and actual truth |
| alignment | phase coherence / minimal deviation | right relationship / balance | functional compliance or harmony | system integrity with feedback awareness |
| truth | consistent model‑to‑reality mapping | relational faithfulness | validated narrative (context‑dependent) | coherence across multiple perspectives |
| power | capacity to change system state | stewardship / responsibility | control or influence over others | ability to maintain balance without collapse |
| justice | equilibrium of forces | moral balance / right action | fairness as perceived by group | equitable distribution with accountability |
| freedom | degrees of freedom in a system | liberation from attachment | autonomy within constraints | capacity to act without coercive restriction |
| distortion | signal deviation / noise | illusion / misperception | manipulation of narrative | systemic misalignment and feedback failure |
| accountability | traceability of state change | responsibility for one’s impact | answerability to those affected | verifiable audit trail with enforceable feedback loops |
| correction | negative feedback to reduce deviation | restoring relational harmony | remediation of harm by responsible party | enforceable mechanism that returns system to alignment |

This schema does not fix meanings. It preserves relational consistency across domains.
